# Writing & Sources

The finished article is written to top-desk standard. The full house style lives in
[`memory/writing-standards.md`](../memory/writing-standards.md); this page is the summary.

## Structure (the WSJ formula)

Anecdotal/scene/surprising-fact **lede** → **nut graf** (why it matters now) → 3–5 **evidence sections**
(named people, contextualized numbers, quotes) → a **steelmanned contrarian turn** → **implications** →
an honest **"What we don't know"** → a **kicker** (forward-looking, not a summary).

## Craft rules

- Active voice; plain words; vary sentence length.
- **Contextualize every number** ("a 15% drop — from 1,100 to 935").
- **Quote** for voice/opinion; **paraphrase** plain facts. Never quote the obvious.
- No clichés / AI-slop tells ("delve", "tapestry", "navigate the landscape", "stands as a testament", …).
- Mandatory contrarian section; mandatory weak-areas section.

## Source hierarchy (credibility-first) — non-negotiable

Cite the strongest available source, in this order:

1. **Primary / official** — government & agency data (BLS, Census, the Fed & regional banks, CBO, GAO,
   SEC/EDGAR, OECD, IMF, World Bank) and official disclosures (10-K, earnings calls, filings).
2. **Peer-reviewed / academic** — journals, NBER, university labs; arXiv marked "preprint".
3. **Market research & consulting** — Gartner, Forrester, IDC, McKinsey, BCG, Bain, Deloitte, CB Insights.
4. **Established journalism** — NYT, WSJ, FT, Reuters, AP, Bloomberg, The Economist, ProPublica.
5. **Everything else** — Substack, blogs, social. Surface leads, then verify against 1–4. **Never load-bearing.**

For any company/industry claim, **triangulate against comparable companies** — don't rely on one firm's
self-report.

## Quote hierarchy (authority-first)

Prefer quotes from: (1) named decision-makers/authorities (a major-org executive, a government official),
(2) the cited academic or named study author, (3) recognized analysts, and only then (4) anonymous/"regular"
individuals — used for lived-experience **color**, clearly framed as one person's anecdote, never as
evidence for a general claim.

## Adversarial verification

Before writing, Stage 1 extracts the 8–12 load-bearing claims and *tries to refute each*, defaulting to
"refuted" when independent support is weak. This is what catches conflated/viral figures (a real $1,200 demo
becoming a fictional "$1.2M budget"; a single tweet becoming a "$500K/month" fact). The verdicts and the
"traps to avoid" are written into `research/verification.md` and obeyed by the writer.

## Voice

The write stage reads your `profile/writing-style.md` (the **house voice**) so the article sounds like you.
A per-article `voice` arg (set in Round 2) overrides it when they conflict. Stance (neutral vs. taking a
side) is separate from voice and is chosen per article in Round 2 — the journalist defaults to neutral.
