# Sources — The AI Job Apocalypse: Is the Fear Real?

Deduplicated and grouped by source-quality tier (1 = anchor → 5 = avoid). Each entry notes the research lens(es) it surfaced from in the shared dossier and its inline citation number [n] in `article.md`. Where a claim's raw operational figures are first-party/self-interested but the *event* is independently corroborated, the split tier is noted.

This article's empirical backbone is unusually Tier-1-heavy for the labor question: the Dallas Fed entry-level/wage finding, the Forrester forecast, the METR productivity RCT, the ILO Philippines study, and the Reuters/Bloomberg walkback-and-valuation facts are all Tier 1–2. The softer ground — Klarna's unaudited savings, the rehire-rate range, the "AI washing" motive, and the displaced-worker testimony — is flagged in the prose and tiered down accordingly.

## Tier 1 — Anchor (peer-reviewed, government, official primary data/forecasts)

**[4] AI is simultaneously aiding and replacing workers, wage data suggest** — Federal Reserve Bank of Dallas, 2026-02-24. https://www.dallasfed.org/research/economics/2026/0224
*Lenses: contrarian, data.* The single most load-bearing source: entry-level substitution vs. experienced complementarity; employment −1% in most-exposed sectors (computer systems design −5%); wages +16.7% vs +7.5% national. Disinterested central-bank research on BLS data.

**[9] Measuring the Impact of Early-2025 AI on Experienced Open-Source Developer Productivity** — Joel Becker, Nate Rush, Beth Barnes & David Rein, METR, 2025-07-10 (arXiv:2507.09089). https://metr.org/blog/2025-07-10-early-2025-ai-experienced-os-dev-study/
*Lens: comparative.* RCT, 16 experienced devs / 246 tasks: −19% productivity (95% CI +2% to +39%) vs +20% perceived. Cited with the authors' own scope limit.

**[12] Forrester: AI-Led Job Disruption Will Escalate, While Fears Of A Job Apocalypse Are Overstated** — Forrester (J.P. Gownder, VP & Principal Analyst), 2026-01-13. https://www.forrester.com/press-newsroom/forrester-impact-ai-jobs-forecast/
*Lenses: expert, follow-the-money.* "Over half" of AI-attributed layoffs reversed; ~6% of US jobs automated by 2030 (~10.4M); the "AI washing" definition.

**[16] OpenAI's Altman says AI unlikely to lead to jobs apocalypse** — Reuters, 2026-05-26. https://www.reuters.com/world/asia-pacific/openais-altman-says-ai-unlikely-lead-jobs-apocalypse-2026-05-26/
*Lens: follow-the-money.* Verbatim "I'm delighted to be wrong about this" (Sydney); the OpenAI ~$1T IPO context. Wire-service primary for the walkback.

**[19] Tracking the impact of AI on the labor market** — Yale Budget Lab, 2026. https://budgetlab.yale.edu/research/tracking-impact-ai-labor-market
*Lens: follow-the-money (competing-explanation source from verification C12).* Tier-1 evidence that little measurable AI displacement has appeared yet — the innocent explanation for the walkbacks.

**[25] Generative AI and jobs in the Philippines: Labour market exposure and policy implications** — Phu Huynh, International Labour Organization, 2026-02-05. https://www.ilo.org/publications/generative-ai-and-jobs-philippines-labour-market-exposure-and-policy
*Lens: comparative.* >25% of jobs (12.7M) exposed but only 3.6% at highest displacement risk — the exposure-≠-replacement keystone for the international contrast.

## Tier 2 — Strong (established journalism / major-firm research)

**[2] AI-driven layoffs aren't generating the returns companies hoped for** — Fortune, 2026-05-11. https://fortune.com/2026/05/11/ai-automation-layoffs-gartner-study-roi/
*Lenses: expert, follow-the-money.* Accessible mirror of the Gartner release; carries the 49,135-AI-layoffs figure and the ~48%-of-tracked-2026-cuts framing.

*Lens: follow-the-money.* The Amodei Jevons reframe; Altman's "whether or not it really is about AI" concession; Amodei's prior 10–20% unemployment / "half of entry-level" warnings.

**[5] As AI layoff regret surges, will boomerang employees make a comeback? (IBM AskHR rehiring; Robert Half / Careerminds cluster)** — HR Executive, 2026-04-21. https://hrexecutive.com/as-ai-layoff-regret-surges-will-boomerang-employees-make-a-comeback/

**[7] Klarna is hiring customer service agents after AI couldn't cut it** — Sherin Shibu, *Entrepreneur* (relaying a Bloomberg interview), 2025-05-09. https://www.entrepreneur.com/business-news/klarna-ceo-reverses-course-by-hiring-more-humans-not-ai/491396
*Lenses: stakeholders, comparative.* Headcount ~4,487 → ~3,500 (~22%); the reversal. Primary is the on-record Bloomberg CEO interview. (Same outlet as [15].)

**[10] AI-led job cuts don't always mean stronger ROI — Gartner** — *Computerworld*, 2026. https://www.computerworld.com/article/4167140/ai-led-job-cuts-dont-always-mean-stronger-roi-gartner.html
*Lens: expert.* Origin of the verbatim Poitevin "no connection or correlation" quote and the "job chaos, not jobs apocalypse" line. Reports the Gartner survey (n=350, $1B+ firms, Q3 2025).

**[11] As AI layoff regret surges, will boomerang employees make a comeback? (Forrester / Robert Half / Careerminds rehire data)** — HR Executive, 2026-04-21. https://hrexecutive.com/as-ai-layoff-regret-surges-will-boomerang-employees-make-a-comeback/
*Lens: stakeholders.* The verbatim Gownder "9 out of 10 times, the answer is no" quote; Robert Half ~29%, Careerminds ~two-thirds rehiring / 20% "launched smoothly." (Survey figures themselves are self-reported — Tier 2–3; attributed to named houses in-text.)

**[13] Marc Andreessen says AI is the "silver bullet excuse" for layoffs driven by overhiring** — *Fortune*, 2026-03-31. https://fortune.com/2026/03/31/marc-andreessen-ai-layoffs-silver-bullet-excuse-overhiring/
*Lens: historical.* The "silver bullet excuse" quote and the 25–75% overstaffing estimate, from a deeply AI-long source (which is what makes the admission notable).

**[17] Anthropic in talks to raise $30 billion at $900 billion valuation** — Bloomberg, 2026-05-12. https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-12/anthropic-in-talks-to-raise-30-billion-at-900-billion-valuation
*Lens: follow-the-money.* The ~$900B Anthropic valuation half of the IPO-timing fact.

**[23] AI Will Reshape More Jobs Than It Replaces** — BCG Henderson Institute, 2026-04. https://www.bcg.com/publications/2026/ai-will-reshape-more-jobs-than-it-replaces
*Lenses: expert, follow-the-money.* Used for its *more bearish* sub-figure (10–15% / 16–25M jobs eliminated within 5 years) beneath the reassuring headline. BCG sells AI-transformation advisory — noted as a conflicted "reshape" beneficiary.

**[24] IT-BPM industry in PH outpaced global growth in 2025 (IBPAP data)** — *Philippine Daily Inquirer*, 2025–2026. https://business.inquirer.net/567026/it-bpm-industry-in-ph-outpaced-global-growth-in-2025
*Lens: comparative.* +4% BPO employment / ~80,000 net new jobs / 67% of firms using AI for productivity. PH paper of record reporting industry-body figures (mild pro-industry optimism noted).

## Tier 3 — Useful, verify (expert Substacks, domain commentators, secondary aggregation of primaries)

**[1] "I was forced to use AI until the day I was laid off."** — Brian Merchant, *Blood in the Machine* (Substack), 2025-12-12. https://www.bloodinthemachine.com/p/i-was-forced-to-use-ai-until-the
*Lens: stakeholders.* The Jacques Reulet II and Marcus Wiesner ($600k/8 staff → <$10k) testimony. Merchant is openly pro-labor and the voices are mostly anonymized — flagged in the "What we don't know" section; used for lived texture, not aggregate proof.

**[6] Klarna's AI assistant handles two-thirds of customer service chats in its first month** — Klarna press release, 2024-02-27. https://www.klarna.com/international/press/klarna-ai-assistant-handles-two-thirds-of-customer-service-chats-in-its-first-month/
*Lens: contrarian.* "700 full-time agents" equivalent; 2.3M conversations; the $40M "estimated profit improvement." First-party and unaudited — interpretation Tier 4, raw figures Tier-2-equivalent; the $40M is forward cost-avoidance, not realized savings (so labeled in-text).

**[8] The Anthropic Economic Index** — Anthropic, 2025-02-10. https://www.anthropic.com/research/the-anthropic-economic-index
*Lens: contrarian.* 57% augmentation / 43% automation; ~4% of occupations using AI for ≥75% of tasks. First-party from an interested lab, but derived from millions of real conversations rather than a survey — flagged accordingly.

**[15] Klarna CEO reverses course, citing "lower quality" AI support (Bloomberg interview)** — *Entrepreneur*, 2025-05-09. https://www.entrepreneur.com/business-news/klarna-ceo-reverses-course-by-hiring-more-humans-not-ai/491396
*Lenses: stakeholders, comparative.* The Siemiatkowski "quality of human support is the way of the future" quote and the hybrid/freelance endpoint. Primary is the Bloomberg interview; CEO has a reputational-repair incentive (noted).

**[18] Billionaires Backpedal on the AI Jobs Apocalypse** — William R. Dodson, *Future Forwarded* (Substack), 2026. https://futureforwarded.substack.com/p/billionaires-backpedal-on-the-ai
*Lens: follow-the-money.* The "catastrophism is bad prospectus material" articulation of the IPO-timing read. Quoted as the *argument*; its factual anchors cross-checked against [16]/[17]. Motive is presented as inference, per verification C12.

**[20] What ATMs & Bank Tellers Reveal About the "Rise of the Robots" (quoting James Bessen)** — AEI / James Pethokoukis, c.2016. https://www.aei.org/economics/what-atms-bank-tellers-rise-robots-and-jobs/
*Lens: historical.* The ATM-teller "augmentation grew jobs" mechanism (21→13 tellers/branch; ~500k→600k tellers). AEI has a pro-technology lean; the Bessen quote is widely reproduced. Paired with [21] so the precedent is shown to cut both ways.

**[21] Why ATMs didn't kill bank teller jobs, but the iPhone did** — David Oks (Substack), c.2023–2024. https://davidoks.blog/p/why-the-atm-didnt-kill-bank-teller
*Lens: historical.* The crucial correction: teller employment fell sharply after ~2010 (smartphone, not ATM) — the "augmentation has an expiry date" point that powers the steelman.

**[22] The Solow Productivity Paradox: What Do Computers Do to Productivity?** — Brookings Institution (quoting Robert Solow, 1987), 1999-03. https://www.brookings.edu/articles/the-solow-productivity-paradox-what-do-computers-do-to-productivity/
*Lens: historical.* The "you can see the computer age everywhere but in the productivity statistics" quote — the ROI/displacement-lag precedent for the steelman. Brookings (Tier 2) carrying a Tier-1 idea (Solow, Nobel laureate). Flagged ⚠OLD by design: cited as precedent, not current measurement.
